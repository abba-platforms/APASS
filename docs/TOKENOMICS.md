Version: 1.1.0  
Status: Official  
Network: BNB Smart Chain (BEP-20 / ERC-20 Compatible)

# 1. Overview

The APASS token is the native economic unit of the Africa Tourism Pass (APASS) protocol.

APASS is structured as a treasury-governed digital asset designed to connect verified tourism activity with a controlled incentive and access system. The token economy is not based on arbitrary issuance or speculative distribution. Instead, token flow is explicitly linked to real-world participation within the tourism ecosystem.

APASS performs four primary functions within the protocol:

- It serves as a reward mechanism issued against verified tourism activity  
- It functions as an access instrument for benefits, discounts, and incentives  
- It operates as a redemption medium within participating tourism networks  
- It is tradable in external markets where liquidity and price discovery occur  

The design objective is to maintain a measurable and controlled relationship between token supply and tourism-driven economic activity.

# 3. Supply Model

The APASS supply model is defined across three distinct layers: maximum supply, minted supply, and unminted supply.

Maximum Supply:
100,000,000,000 APASS  

This represents the absolute cap. Under no circumstances will total supply exceed this amount.

Minted Supply:
1,000,000,000 APASS  

This represents the total number of tokens currently created on-chain.

Unminted Supply:
99,000,000,000 APASS  

This portion remains unissued and can only be minted through authorized governance processes.

Minting is not continuous or algorithmic. It is controlled, discrete, and policy-driven.

---

# 4. Treasury Custody Model

All minted tokens are initially allocated to the protocol treasury.

The treasury functions as:

- The sole origin point of all token distribution  
- The custody layer for undistributed supply  
- The control layer for allocation enforcement  

No tokens enter circulation without passing through treasury-controlled mechanisms.

Treasury responsibilities include:

- Managing distribution flows  
- Enforcing allocation boundaries  
- Maintaining auditability of all token movements  
- Preserving separation between allocation categories  

This structure ensures that token issuance, distribution, and deployment remain governed and traceable.

---

# 5. Distribution Model

Distribution refers to tokens that have been transferred or allocated out of treasury under authorized processes.

Total Distributed Supply:
190,000,000 APASS  

This represents the portion of minted supply that has been released from treasury into defined allocation pathways or external ownership.

Distribution Breakdown:

100,000,000 APASS allocated to the Admin Wallet  

This allocation is controlled and not considered public circulating supply unless transferred externally.

1,700,000 APASS distributed to travelers  

These tokens are held by external users and represent actual ecosystem-level circulation.

88,300,000 APASS allocated within treasury-controlled distribution pathways  

These tokens are designated for operational deployment, including incentives and market-related functions, but remain under structured control.

Total:
190,000,000 APASS

# 6. Treasury Balance

Remaining Treasury Balance:
810,000,000 APASS  

This represents minted tokens that have not yet been distributed.

These tokens remain:

- Non-circulating  
- Under full treasury control  
- Available for future allocation based on governance decisions  

No portion of this balance is released without explicit authorization.

# 8. Market Operations and Exchange Liquidity

A portion of tokens is designated for market operations.

Market Operations Target:
190,000,000 APASS  

Within this allocation:

70,000,000 APASS is reserved for centralized exchange liquidity provisioning and secondary market support  

This reserve is:

- Held under treasury-controlled allocation  
- Not considered circulating until transferred externally  
- Not classified as rewards distribution  

The function of this reserve includes:

- Supporting centralized exchange listings  
- Providing liquidity depth  
- Enabling market maker participation  
- Stabilizing order books  
- Supporting price discovery  

No tokens are considered deployed to exchanges until explicitly transferred to exchange-controlled wallets.

# 9. Founder and Team Allocation

A defined allocation is reserved for founders and core team members.

This allocation is:

- Governed through treasury-controlled issuance  
- Subject to vesting schedules  
- Released in phases aligned with ecosystem milestones  

The purpose is to align long-term incentives with protocol growth and prevent early supply concentration in markets.

# 10. Rewards Model

APASS rewards are issued based on verified tourism activity.

Rewards issuance is not discretionary and is governed by defined rules.

Characteristics:

- Rewards are issued only upon verified activity  
- Issuance is tied to measurable participation  
- No arbitrary emissions occur  

Control parameters include:

- Daily issuance limits  
- Per-user limits  
- Merchant-level controls  
- Time-based restrictions  

This ensures that rewards distribution remains controlled and proportional to real activity.

---

# 11. Redemption Model

APASS tokens function as a benefit and access mechanism within the ecosystem.

Tokens can be used to obtain:

- Discounts on accommodation  
- Travel-related incentives  
- Partner-based benefits  

The economic cycle is structured as:

Participation → Reward issuance → Token access → Redemption → Continued participation  

This creates a continuous engagement loop linking activity to value.

# 12. Circulating Supply Definition

Circulating supply is defined strictly as:

Tokens held outside treasury control  

This includes:

- Tokens held by travelers  
- Tokens held by external market participants  
- Tokens deployed to exchanges  

This excludes:

- Treasury-held tokens  
- Reserved but undeployed allocations  
- Admin-controlled tokens not released externally  

This definition ensures clarity in supply measurement.
