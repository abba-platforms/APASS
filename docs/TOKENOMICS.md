# APASS Tokenomics

Version: 1.0.0  
Status: Official  
Network: BNB Smart Chain (BEP-20 / ERC-20 Compatible)

---

# 1. Overview

The APASS token is the native economic unit of the Africa Tourism Pass (APASS) protocol.

It functions as:

- A reward mechanism for verified tourism participation  
- A redemption medium for tourism services  
- A tradable digital asset across external markets  

The token economy is governed through a treasury-controlled issuance and distribution model designed to ensure long-term sustainability, controlled circulation, and institutional transparency.

---

# 2. Token Standard

The APASS token is:

- BEP-20 native  
- ERC-20 compatible  
- Deployed on BNB Smart Chain  

This ensures interoperability with wallets, exchanges, and smart contract integrations.

---

# 3. Total Supply and Issuance Model

Maximum Supply: 100,000,000,000 APASS  
Initial Minted Supply: 1,000,000,000 APASS  

Token issuance is controlled through protocol-level minting functions and restricted to authorized roles.

Additional minting may occur over time, subject to governance and treasury policy, without exceeding the maximum supply cap.

---

# 4. Initial Treasury Allocation

The initial minted supply of 1,000,000,000 APASS is allocated entirely to the protocol treasury.

The treasury acts as the sole source of token distribution across the ecosystem.

No tokens are distributed outside of treasury-controlled mechanisms.

---

# 5. Treasury Allocation Framework

The APASS protocol defines structured allocation targets within the treasury.

These represent planned distribution capacity, not immediate circulating supply.

Defined Allocation Targets:

Rewards Pool  
Target Allocation: 5,000,000,000 APASS  

Burn Compensation Pool  
Target Allocation: 1,000,000,000 APASS  

Market Operations Reserve  
Allocation: 190,000,000 APASS  

Founders and Core Team  
Target Allocation: 10,000,000,000 APASS  

Ecosystem and Strategic Growth  
Target Allocation: 20,000,000,000 APASS  

Unallocated Reserve  
Remaining supply reserved for future issuance and protocol expansion  

---

# 6. Founder and Team Allocation

A dedicated allocation is reserved for founders and core team members.

Key principles:

- Issued through treasury governance  
- Subject to phased release or vesting schedules  
- Designed to align long-term incentives with protocol growth  

This ensures commitment to long-term ecosystem development.

---

# 7. Market Operations Allocation

The APASS protocol allocates a portion of tokens for market operations.

Allocation: 190,000,000 APASS  

Purpose:

- Centralized exchange (CEX) listings  
- Market maker provisioning  
- Liquidity pool seeding  
- Order book stabilization  
- Initial trading support  

This allocation is strictly separated from rewards distribution.

---

# 8. Active Allocation State

At current deployment:

- Total minted supply: 1,000,000,000 APASS  
- Treasury holds full minted supply  
- Rewards Pool allocated: 190,000,000 APASS (initial funding)  
- Market Operations Reserve defined within treasury  
- Remaining allocations pending phased issuance  

This ensures controlled and gradual token release.

---

# 9. Distribution Flow

All token distribution follows a strict treasury-controlled path:

Minting → Treasury → Allocation Pools → Authorized Distribution → End Users / Markets

No direct external distribution bypassing treasury is permitted.

---

# 10. Circulating Supply Definition

Circulating supply is defined as:

Tokens distributed from treasury to external wallets.

At initial deployment:

- Circulating supply is limited  
- Majority of tokens remain in treasury custody  

This prevents uncontrolled market flooding.

---

# 11. Rewards Model

APASS operates a verification-driven rewards system.

Characteristics:

- Rewards issued only upon verified tourism activity  
- Governed by protocol rules  
- Subject to limits and controls  

Constraints include:

- Daily limits  
- Per-user limits  
- Merchant-level limits  
- Time-based restrictions  

---

# 12. Redemption Model

APASS tokens can be redeemed for tourism services.

This creates a closed-loop system:

Participation → Rewards → Redemption → Continued Engagement
