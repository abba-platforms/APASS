# APASS Merchant Onboarding

## 1. Overview

Onboarding merchants onto the Africa Tourism Pass (APASS) network is free of charge.

APASS is designed to enable tourism operators to participate in a coordinated ecosystem where tourism activity is verified, incentivized, and digitally recorded without requiring changes to existing payment systems.

Merchants retain full control over their pricing, payment methods, and operations.

---

## 2. Who Can Join

APASS is open to tourism-related businesses located anywhere in Africa and beyond, including:

- Lodges, motels, and hotels
- Guesthouses and accommodations
- Tour operators and guides
- Transportation providers
- Cultural and tourism activity providers

Participation is voluntary and based on merchant readiness to offer incentives to APASS users.

---

## 3. Onboarding Process

The onboarding process is structured, simple, and does not require technical expertise.

### Onboarding Flow (Illustrative)

```text
Merchant
   │
   │  Registration
   ▼
+------------------------+
|   APASS Platform       |
|   (Merchant Intake)    |
+------------------------+
   │
   │  Verification
   ▼
+------------------------+
|   Verification Layer   |
| (KYC / Business Check) |
+------------------------+
   │
   │  Approved
   ▼
+------------------------+
| Participation Setup    |
| (Discounts / Methods)  |
+------------------------+
   │
   │  Activation
   ▼
+------------------------+
|   APASS Network        |
| (Live Merchant Listed) |
+------------------------+
   │
   │  Traveler Interaction
   ▼
+------------------------+
| Verification + Rewards |
+------------------------+
```

### Step 1: Registration

The merchant registers through the APASS platform by providing:

- Business name
- Location
- Contact information
- Type of tourism service

---

### Step 2: Verification

The merchant is verified to ensure legitimacy and operational status.

This may include:

- Business registration validation
- Identity verification
- Location confirmation

Only verified merchants are activated on the APASS network.

---

### Step 3: Participation Setup

The merchant defines participation parameters, including:

- Discount or incentive offerings (optional but recommended)
- Supported verification method (QR or POS-based interaction)
- Service categories offered

Merchants are not required to change their pricing models.

---

### Step 4: Activation

Once verified and configured:

- The merchant is listed within the APASS network
- Travelers can identify and interact with the merchant
- Verification and reward mechanisms become active

---

## 4. How Transactions Work

APASS does not process payments.

Merchants continue to accept payments through existing methods, including:

- Credit and debit cards
- Mobile payments
- Bank transfers
- Cash
- Digital settlement systems such as NADD where available

APASS operates as a separate verification and incentive layer.

---

## 5. Verification and Rewards

When a traveler interacts with a participating merchant:

- The transaction is verified via QR scan or POS interaction
- The activity is recorded within the APASS system
- Reward points are issued to the traveler
- Incentives or discounts are applied as defined by the merchant

Verification ensures that rewards are tied to real tourism activity.

---

## 6. Merchant Benefits

Merchants benefit from participation through:

- Increased visibility within the APASS network
- Access to a broader traveler base
- Higher customer engagement and repeat visits
- Participation in a coordinated tourism ecosystem
- Access to structured tourism activity data

APASS functions as a demand-generation and coordination layer rather than a payment intermediary.

## 7. Cost Structure

- Merchant onboarding is free
- There are no mandatory integration fees
- Merchants are not required to adopt new payment systems

Participation costs are limited to optional incentives or discounts offered to travelers.

---

## 8. Technical Requirements

APASS is designed for minimal technical overhead.

Merchants require:

- A mobile device or POS interface for verification
- Basic connectivity for transaction validation

No complex blockchain interaction is required from the merchant.

---

## 9. Compliance and Responsibility

Merchants are responsible for:

- Maintaining accurate business information
- Delivering agreed services and incentives
- Complying with local regulations

APASS provides verification and coordination but does not assume operational control of merchant services.

---

## 10. Service and Support Expectations

APASS is designed to provide a reliable verification and incentive layer for participating merchants.

Operational expectations include:

- Verification services operating on a continuous basis, subject to network and system availability
- Administrative support for merchant activation, updates, and participation management
- Best-effort issue response for onboarding, verification, and participation-related questions

APASS does not guarantee uninterrupted external connectivity, payment network availability, or merchant-side device uptime.

---

## 11. Dispute Resolution

Where a merchant or traveler identifies an issue relating to verification, rewards, or recorded participation, the matter may be reviewed through APASS support and dispute-handling procedures.

Examples include:

- Verified activity not recorded correctly
- Reward points not issued as expected
- Duplicate or failed verification attempts
- Disagreement regarding discount application or participation status

Dispute resolution is handled through protocol-defined administrative processes and, where applicable, supporting system components such as the dispute registry.

APASS does not adjudicate the underlying commercial performance of tourism services delivered by merchants.

---

## 12. Data Privacy and Handling

APASS records tourism participation data in a structured and auditable manner.

Data handling principles include:

- Recording only the information required for verification, rewards, and system integrity
- Avoiding unnecessary exposure of personally identifiable information on-chain
- Maintaining a separation between operational records and sensitive user data where applicable
- Using verifiable transaction records for ecosystem measurement and analytics

Merchants are expected to comply with applicable privacy and data-handling requirements in their jurisdiction.

---

## 13. Merchant Suspension and Offboarding

Merchants may be suspended, restricted, or removed from the APASS network under defined conditions.

These may include:

- False or misleading registration information
- Fraudulent verification activity
- Repeated failure to honor defined participation terms
- Regulatory or legal non-compliance
- Operational inactivity or administrative closure

Suspension or removal is performed through authorized administrative processes under the governance and control framework of the APASS protocol.

---

## 14. Incentive Policy Boundaries

Discounts, promotions, and incentive structures within the APASS network are subject to defined boundaries.

- Merchants solely determine the discounts or incentives they choose to offer
- APASS does not guarantee any specific merchant revenue outcome
- Reward issuance remains subject to protocol rules, verification validity, and system controls
- Participation in APASS does not create a guaranteed customer volume obligation

This ensures the incentive model remains sustainable, transparent, and operationally manageable.

---

## 15. Geographic Scope and Expansion

APASS is designed for deployment across Africa, with phased activation based on market readiness, merchant participation, and ecosystem coordination.

Initial onboarding may begin within selected markets or corridors and expand progressively over time.

The protocol is designed to remain compatible across multiple jurisdictions and does not depend on any single payment method or currency framework.

Where integrated digital settlement options such as NADD are available, they may operate alongside other payment methods without altering the core APASS merchant onboarding model.

---

## 16. Integration Path

Merchant participation in APASS may evolve over time from basic verification to deeper operational integration.

Potential integration paths include:

- QR-based traveler verification
- POS-assisted interaction recording
- Wallet-based participation confirmation
- API-based integrations for larger operators or platforms
- Expanded compatibility with external systems and settlement layers

This allows merchants to begin with minimal requirements and scale participation as operational readiness increases.

---

## 17. Legal and Operational Positioning

APASS is a verification and incentive protocol.

It is not:

- A payment processor
- A bank
- A financial intermediary
- A replacement for merchant operational systems

Merchants remain fully independent businesses and retain responsibility for:

- Their pricing
- Their services
- Their customer relationships
- Their regulatory obligations

APASS provides coordination, verification, and incentive infrastructure only.

---

## 18. Summary

APASS enables merchants to participate in a modern, coordinated tourism ecosystem without disrupting existing operations.

- No onboarding cost
- No payment system replacement
- No technical complexity beyond basic verification capability
- Direct access to incentivized tourism demand
- Structured participation within a verifiable tourism network

Merchants remain independent while benefiting from a coordinated and growing tourism ecosystem.
